# Do Not Claim List — Sahid Attaf

Read this before every resume edit, cover letter draft, or interview preparation session.
Updated: 2026-06-23

---

## The Rule

Every claim on a resume, in a cover letter, or in an interview must be:
1. Verifiable from the resume, candidate profile, or GitHub.
2. Defensible if an interviewer asks a specific follow-up question.
3. True as of the date of the application, not anticipated future state.

If a claim fails any of these tests, do not make it.

## What Sahid Must Not Claim Yet

**Do not say:** "I have a degree in..." / "I studied at..." / "I hold a bachelor's..." /
"I have a master's in..." / any institution name unless a degree was earned there.

**Safe alternative:** "I do not hold a traditional degree. I bring five years of
practical AI workflow and business automation experience, course-based training in
Claude and Gemini, and a working Python project I can demo live."

**Why:** No degree has been confirmed. The Coursiv certificates are course certificates,
not degrees. Claiming otherwise is a factual error that will surface immediately in any
background check or direct question.

### 8. A live demo unless the demo is ready

**Do not say:** "I can demo this anytime" or "the demo is ready" unless:
- Pack 07 demo pre-checklist is fully checked.
- Three rehearsals have been completed and logged.
- The practice package generates without errors.
- The package reviewer returns READY_FOR_HUMAN_REVIEW.

**Safe alternative (current):** "I have a working system. It's not in demo-ready
condition right now, but I can prepare a live walkthrough with a week's notice."

**Why:** A failed demo in an interview is worse than no demo. Don't promise
what isn't ready.

### 9. Customer success or TAM experience

**Do not say:** "I have customer success experience" / "I've worked as a TAM" /
"I've managed enterprise accounts."

**Safe alternative:** "I haven't held a formal CSM or TAM title. I've worked
directly with clients in a hospitality and business consulting context — managing
expectations, resolving problems, and delivering outcomes. I understand the CS
framework conceptually and I'm building the specific vocabulary for enterprise
customer success roles."

**Why:** No CSM or TAM role in the resume. Hospitality operations experience is
genuinely transferable but is not the same thing.

---

### 10. Quantified outcomes not verified by Sahid

**Do not say:** "Increased revenue by X%" / "Reduced time by Y%" /
"Served Z clients" / any metric unless Sahid can cite where it came from.

**Safe alternative:** Describe what was built and what it does. Let the interviewer
draw their own inferences about impact. "I built a system that does X for Y purpose"
is always safe. "I increased Y by Z%" is only safe if the number is real.
