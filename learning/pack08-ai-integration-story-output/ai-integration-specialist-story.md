# AI Integration Specialist Story — Sahid Attaf

Three timed versions of the core narrative. Practice each until the timing
is natural — not rushed, not padded. Specific is credible. Long is forgettable.

---

## Version A — 30 Seconds

Use for: First impression, phone screen opener, LinkedIn connection note.

---

"I'm an AI systems builder based in Curaçao. Over the past five years I've
designed and built AI-powered workflows for business automation — my clearest
example is JobHunterGPT, a human-in-the-loop Python pipeline I built from
scratch that covers keyword extraction, fit scoring, cover letter generation,
and output review. I don't have enterprise platform experience yet, but I
understand the workflow design pattern and I can show you the work directly."

---

**Word count:** ~75 words. Spoken time: 28–32 seconds.

**Every claim verified:**
- "AI systems builder" — GPT Innovation by Attaf role
- "five years" — confirmed
- "AI-powered workflows for business automation" — resume
- "JobHunterGPT... human-in-the-loop Python pipeline" — verified, local
- "keyword extraction, fit scoring, cover letter generation, output review" — real modules
- "don't have enterprise platform experience yet" — honest
- "I can show you the work" — demo is scripted and ready

**Practice note:** The phrase "human-in-the-loop" and "I can show you the
work directly" are the two anchors. If you forget the middle, say those two
things and you've communicated the most important points.

## Version B — 60 Seconds

Use for: Early interview opener, portfolio introduction, video application.

---

"I'm an AI systems builder and entrepreneur based in Curaçao. Over the past
five years I've focused on translating real business problems into practical,
multi-step AI workflow solutions — for hospitality operations, real estate
development, and small business automation.

The project I'm most concrete about is JobHunterGPT — a human-in-the-loop,
Python-based AI pipeline I built from scratch covering keyword extraction, ATS
fit scoring, cover letter generation, and structural package review before any
file is used. It runs on Python's standard library, has 70 automated tests,
and never submits anything automatically. I can demo it live.

My background doesn't include enterprise platforms like Salesforce or Amazon Q —
I want to be upfront about that. What I bring is the workflow design thinking,
the ability to build and test AI systems independently, and in June 2026 I
completed structured AI tool training in Claude and Gemini through Coursiv.
I'm actively building the enterprise-specific knowledge this kind of role requires."

---

**Word count:** ~155 words. Spoken time: 58–65 seconds.

**Structure:**
1. Who you are + domain (sentence 1)
2. What you do — pattern and domains (sentence 2)
3. Specific proof — JobHunterGPT details (paragraph 2)
4. Honest gap acknowledgment — before they ask (paragraph 3)
5. What you bring and what you're doing about the gap (paragraph 3 continued)

**Practice note:** Paragraph 3 is the gap acknowledgment. Say it calmly,
without apology, and without trailing off. The goal is to make the interviewer
feel they can trust everything else you say. Practice saying "I want to be
upfront about that" without sounding defensive.

## Version C — 90 Seconds

Use for: Full interview opener ("Tell me about yourself"), cover letter
opening, LinkedIn About section introduction.

---

"I'm an AI systems builder and entrepreneur based in Curaçao. For the past
five years my work has been about one thing: taking real business problems —
in hospitality operations, real estate development, marketing, and small
business automation — and building AI-powered workflows that solve them
in a practical, verifiable way.

The clearest example of that is JobHunterGPT. It's a local Python prototype
I built from the ground up — eight modules, 70 automated tests, human
review at every output step. It handles the full preparation pipeline for
a job application: keyword extraction from the job description, ATS fit
scoring against the candidate's profile, cover letter generation from verified
facts only, and a structural reviewer that checks the output before it's
used. It never submits anything automatically. Every claim in the output has
to be backed by the source resume, or the system flags it. I can run this
live in a screen share in about five minutes.

I also completed structured AI tool training in Claude and Gemini through
Coursiv in June 2026 — nine hours total — and I actively work with Python,
Claude Code, GitHub, and VS Code as my daily tools.

I want to be upfront about what I don't have: I haven't worked inside an
enterprise platform at scale. I don't have Salesforce or Jira hands-on
experience yet. I don't have a cybersecurity background. Those are real
gaps for a role like this, and I'm not going to pretend otherwise.

What I'd ask you to weigh is the design thinking and the trajectory. I
understand why human-in-the-loop matters in AI workflows. I understand the
difference between a system that generates output and a system that validates
it. And I'm the kind of person who builds the system, tests it, breaks it,
and fixes it — not someone who reads about it. That instinct transfers."

---

**Word count:** ~275 words. Spoken time: 85–95 seconds.

**Structure:**
1. Identity and domain (paragraph 1)
2. Specific proof — full JobHunterGPT description (paragraph 2)
3. Tools and certificates (paragraph 3)
4. Honest gap acknowledgment — named specifically (paragraph 4)
5. The ask — what to weigh, what transfers (paragraph 5)

**Practice note:** Paragraph 4 is the hardest to say without flinching.
Practice it as a statement of fact, not a confession. "Those are real gaps
and I'm not going to pretend otherwise" should sound like confidence, not
apology. The interviewer will trust everything before and after it more
because you said it.

## The Honest Gap Acknowledgment (Standalone)

For any moment in an interview when the gap question comes up unexpectedly:

"Before I walk you through my background, I want to be upfront: I come from
a non-traditional path. I've built real AI systems, but I haven't worked inside
an enterprise at scale. I don't have Salesforce or Jira hands-on experience yet,
though I've been actively studying both. I can tell you exactly what I bring and
what I'd need to ramp up on — I think that honesty is more useful to you than
a polished pitch that glosses over the gaps."

Practice until it sounds calm and natural. This is not a weakness statement —
it is a trust statement.
