## Skill Goal

Write and rehearse the narrative version of your candidacy. Not a list of skills —
a story. One that explains who you are, what you've built, what you can do, and
why this role is a natural next step — in a way that is 100% true and defensible.

This is the hardest pack and the most important one. Everything else feeds into it.

---

## Why It Matters

Enterprise hiring decisions are made in conversations, not on paper.
A candidate who can tell a clear, specific, honest story about their background
wins over a candidate with a better resume who can't explain themselves in an interview.

Sahid's background is genuinely unusual:

- Self-taught AI systems builder in the Caribbean.
- Ran hospitality operations for a real restaurant.
- Built real estate projects and AI workflow tools in parallel.
- Currently building the exact type of AI automation system (JobHunterGPT) that
  enterprise companies like Proofpoint want their AI platform to do.

That is a real story. It needs to be told precisely, not hidden behind generic phrases.

---

## The Story Problem to Solve

The gap between Sahid's background and an enterprise AI Integration Specialist role
is real. The story does not pretend the gap doesn't exist. Instead it does three things:

1. Establishes what is genuinely strong and specific (the AI builder identity).
2. Demonstrates active learning (the 8-pack project — honest gap-closing).
3. Makes a credible argument for why the transferable skills and the learning
   trajectory make this a reasonable next step.

An interviewer who hears this story should think: "Not the conventional candidate,
but I understand exactly what they bring and what they'd need to ramp up on."
Not: "I have no idea what this person does."
Not: "This person is claiming experience they don't have."

---

## The Core Narrative Structure

Draft 1 should answer all five of these questions in 90 seconds or less:

1. Who are you professionally? (One sentence.)
2. What have you built that is real and specific? (Two sentences, named projects.)
3. What is your technical depth? (One sentence — honest, specific tools.)
4. What is your honest gap and what are you doing about it? (Two sentences.)
5. Why this role? (One sentence — specific to AI Integration Specialist work.)

Total: ~90 seconds. Not shorter — specific is credible. Not longer — attention is limited.

## The Honest Gap Acknowledgment Script

This is a specific phrase to practice for every interview:

"Before I walk you through my background, I want to be upfront: I come from
a non-traditional path. I've built real AI systems, but I haven't worked inside
an enterprise at scale. I don't have Salesforce or Jira hands-on experience yet,
though I've been actively studying both. I can tell you exactly what I bring and
what I'd need to ramp up on — I think that honesty is more useful to you than
a polished pitch that glosses over the gaps."

Practice this until it sounds calm, not defensive. The goal is to make the
interviewer feel they can trust the rest of what you say.
