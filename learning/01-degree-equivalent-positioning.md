## Why It Matters for Proofpoint-Style Roles

Proofpoint's requirement: "4-year college degree in Computer Science, Engineering,
Business, or a related field, OR EQUIVALENT EXPERIENCE."

The "or equivalent experience" clause is a genuine opening — not a loophole. Many
enterprise tech companies use it to reach non-traditional candidates who have built
real things. Proofpoint specifically says it believes in "culture-add" and "encourages
people from all walks of life to apply."

However: the equivalent experience must be real, datable, and describable. Vague
claims will fall apart in an interview. A specific, honest answer is far stronger
than a confident-sounding one that cannot be backed up.

## Questions to Answer Before This Pack Is Complete

Answer each question honestly. Record your answers in `config/candidate_profile.json`
under a new `education` field once confirmed.

1. Have you completed any formal degree program (associate, bachelor, or higher)?
   If yes: What field? What institution? What year?

2. Have you completed any formal courses, bootcamps, or certifications?
   If yes: What? Where? When? Is there a certificate or record?

3. When did you begin working professionally in AI, technology, or business consulting?
   What is the earliest project or role you can point to with a date?

4. Can you name 5 years' worth of work that a hiring manager could trace through
   your resume, LinkedIn profile, or GitHub commit history?

5. Are you comfortable stating "equivalent experience" on a job application?
   If no, why? (This is worth working through before any submission.)
