# Pack 02 — Salesforce Basics

**Status:** Not Started
**Honest starting position:** No Salesforce experience.

---

## Skill Goal

Understand what Salesforce is, how it is used in Customer Office and AI integration
workflows, and be able to speak intelligently about CRM concepts in an interview
without claiming hands-on experience you do not have.

Build a mock CRM structure in Google Sheets or CSV that demonstrates you understand
the data model Salesforce manages — enough to explain how an AI platform would
connect to it.

---

## Why It Matters for Proofpoint-Style Roles

The Proofpoint JD named Salesforce explicitly as a data source that the AI platform
(Amazon Quick) must connect to:

  "Connect, shape, and manage data pipelines between business systems such as
   Salesforce, Totango, Jira, Redshift, and Snowflake and the AI platform."

A candidate who cannot speak to what Salesforce holds and why it matters to customer
workflows will struggle in a technical interview for this role.

## Three Learning Tasks

**Task 1: Learn what Salesforce holds.**
Study the core Salesforce objects: Account, Contact, Lead, Opportunity, Case,
Activity, Task, Campaign. Understand what each object stores and how they relate.
Free resource: Salesforce Trailhead → "Salesforce Platform Basics" (free, no account
required to read most content).
Write a plain-English description of each object (1-2 sentences each).
Save as: `learning/evidence/02-salesforce-objects.txt`

**Task 2: Understand how AI connects to Salesforce.**
Research: What does it mean to pull data from Salesforce into an AI platform?
- What is the Salesforce API? (REST API, SOQL queries)
- What is a data connector in the context of an AI knowledge base?
- What fields from Salesforce would a Customer Success AI agent need?
Write 5 bullet points summarizing what you learned.
Save as: `learning/evidence/02-salesforce-ai-integration-notes.txt`

**Task 3: Build a mock CRM table.**
Create a Google Sheets file (or CSV file saved in `learning/evidence/`) that mimics
the structure of a basic Salesforce customer table.

Required columns:
  Account ID | Account Name | Contact Name | Contact Email | Stage | Health Score |
  Last Activity Date | Next Action | Owner | Notes

Add 5 fictional (clearly labelled as fictional) records.
This demonstrates you understand the data model without claiming Salesforce access.
Save as: `learning/evidence/02-mock-crm-table.csv`

## Mini Demo Task

In a practice interview setting, answer this question out loud:
"Have you worked with Salesforce before?"

Honest answer to practice:
"I haven't used Salesforce directly, but I understand the CRM data model it manages —
accounts, contacts, opportunities, cases, and activity history. As part of preparing
for roles like this, I built a mock CRM structure to understand how that data would
connect to an AI platform for customer success workflows. I'm confident I can get
up to speed on Salesforce quickly given how its data model maps to systems I've
already worked with."

Practice until it sounds natural and not defensive.
