# Pack 06 — Customer Success / Technical Account Management (TAM)

**Status:** Not Started
**Honest starting position:** No formal customer success or TAM experience.
**Target level:** Conceptual fluency — enough to speak the language and demonstrate
transferable skills from hospitality operations and client-facing business work.

---

## Skill Goal

Learn the vocabulary, frameworks, and mindset of customer success and technical
account management. Map Sahid's verified hospitality operations and client-facing
business experience onto the CS/TAM conceptual model so that transferable experience
can be described accurately in an interview.

---

## Why It Matters for Proofpoint-Style Roles

Proofpoint's JD: "Partner directly with Proofpoint's largest enterprise accounts and
the Strategic Customer Success Manager team... Conduct regular business reviews (QBRs)
with account teams. Serve as the technical and strategic voice for AI within the
customer office."

This is fundamentally a customer-facing role. The candidate must understand:
- What a QBR is and what goes into one.
- What "customer success" means as a profession (vs. sales or support).
- How technical account managers communicate value to enterprise clients.
- What "health scores," "adoption metrics," and "renewal risk" mean.

A candidate who says "I worked in hospitality — similar customer focus" without
understanding the CS framework will seem unprepared. Understanding the framework
allows honest transferable skills to be articulated precisely.

## Three Learning Tasks

**Task 1: Learn the Customer Success vocabulary.**
Study these terms and write a plain-English definition for each:

  Customer Success | Technical Account Manager (TAM) | Quarterly Business Review (QBR)
  Health Score | Churn | NPS (Net Promoter Score) | Time-to-Value (TTV)
  Onboarding | Adoption | Renewal | Upsell / Expansion | Champion | Sponsor
  Success Plan | Use Case | ROI | Executive Sponsor | Escalation Path

Free resources:
  - Gainsight's blog (gainsight.com/blog) — free, no account required
  - Customer Success Association (customersuccessassociation.com)
  - LinkedIn Learning "Customer Success Management Fundamentals" (check free access)

Save as: `learning/evidence/06-cs-vocabulary.txt`

**Task 2: Map Sahid's experience to the CS framework.**
Write a 1-page mapping document showing how each verified experience area
connects to a Customer Success concept.

Format:
  Verified experience: Managing guest operations and complaints at BOSSA Asado i Mar
  CS equivalent: Reactive customer success — resolving issues to protect retention
  Honest gap: Scale (20 covers vs. enterprise accounts), SaaS context, metrics

  Verified experience: Building AI workflow automation for business clients
  CS equivalent: Technical implementation, onboarding, value realization
  Honest gap: No recurring account management cycle, no renewal/churn context

  Verified experience: SOP creation and staff training for hospitality operations
  CS equivalent: Playbook development and enablement
  Honest gap: No enterprise documentation at scale

Save as: `learning/evidence/06-experience-mapping.txt`

## Mini Demo Task

In a practice interview setting, answer this question:
"Tell me about a time you managed a difficult client relationship."

Use a real example from BOSSA or another verified experience.
Structure: Situation → What you did → Outcome → What you learned.
Do not invent clients or situations.

Practice the answer until it is under 2 minutes and sounds conversational.
